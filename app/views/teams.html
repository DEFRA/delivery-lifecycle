Defra service delivery lifecycle teams

The delivery lifecycle is based on the Service Manual, which provides most of the guidance that you will need on setting up a service team.

It describes multidisciplinary service teams that work together to deliver services in accordance with the Service Standard.

This section builds on that information in the Service Manual to describe how these teams are organised in Defra.

We describe three different types of team.

Vertical service teams

Vertical service teams are delivering a service that is closely aligned to a unique government capability.

Examples include teams working on services like:

  1. buy a rod fishing licence
  2. find out if you're at risk of flooding
  3. test for diseases in farm animals

Horizontal service teams

Horizontal service teams are delivering a service that is common across government, or at least common across Defra.

Examples include teams working on services like:

  1. customer service
  2. field workforce
  3. identity management

Functional teams

Functional teams provide capabilities that are required by the multidisciplinary service teams, but these capabilities are coalesced into central teams for reasons of efficiency and effectiveness.

Examples include teams that provide capability like:

  1. web operations
  2. portfolio management
  3. information assurance
