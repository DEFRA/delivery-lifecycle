Public beta support design

Following a successful assessment the Service Team will open up their service for general use in a Public Beta.

This phase focuses on transitioning from continuous change to continuous improvement.

Some of the responsibilities of the Service Team will transfer to functional teams and some of the people in the Service Team may move to focus on other services.

As this transition takes place and as the Service Team respond to operation of their service some of the support arrangements will change. The Service Design function will continue to work with the Service Team and the Operations Management function to refine the support arrangements during the Public Beta.

Without this interaction, the Service Team will be unable to transition to a continuous improvement state.

Teams

These are the teams that are involved in this interaction and what they want to achieve.

Service team

"I want my service to be supported in Public Beta."

"I want to identify, understand and resolve issues arising from operating at full scale."

"I want to scale down the team effort."

Support design team

"I want to understand how the support arrangements work in practice."

"I want to ensure that the support levels will be maintained."

Operations management team

"I want to acquire the support knowledge to manage the applications."

"I want to ensure that the support levels will be maintained."

Objectives

The objective of this interaction is to ensure that the service will continue to be supported during Public Beta in such a way that the service can progress to Live.

Activities

The service team, operations management function and support design function will work together on the following activities.

Continuous change

Adjusting the mechanisms for continous change.

Operational readiness review

Checking that the operational environment is ready for Live.

This forms part of the Live assessment criteria.

Timing

This interaction should be started a little while after the beginning of the Public Beta. This is to allow time for the Operations Management function to have taken on responsibility for the applications and to have gathered some experience of the service operating at scale.
