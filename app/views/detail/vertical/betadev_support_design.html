Beta support design

During Beta the Service Team will iteratively build the applications that form their service. The Support Design team will work with the Service Team to build the corresponding support capability.

There will be separate designs for Private and Public Beta and an assessment of support readiness for Private Beta.

Without this interaction, the Service Team will be unable to proceed with a Private or Public Beta.

Teams

These are the teams that are involved in this interaction and what they want to achieve.

Service team

"I want my service to be supported in Private and Public Beta."

"I want to iterate my build."

Support design team

"I want to ensure that the service is supportable."

"I want to ensure that the service will be supported in Private Beta."

Objectives

The objective of this interaction is to ensure that the service can proceed to Private Beta.

Activities

The service team and support design function will work together on the following activities.

Workflow definition

Defining the support workflow for incident handling during Private Beta.

Operating level agreement

Defining the support levels to be provided during Private Beta.

Continuous change

Preparing the operational environment for continuous change.

Operational readiness review

Checking that the operational environment is ready for Private Beta.

Timing

This interaction should be started once the Service Team have begun development. This is so that the team have been able to explore how their service might work well enough to be able to understand what some of the support needs will be.
