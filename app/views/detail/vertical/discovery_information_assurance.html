Information assurance

Before starting Alpha, the Service Team will need to establish the security characteristics of the information that the service will need to handle.

The Information Assurance function are well placed to help the Service Team identify this information. They will be able to help identify the key security needs, in particular any special measures that might need to be taken during Alpha.

Without this interaction, the investigation in Alpha could be wasted as it would be based on erroneous assumptions and could even, in the worst cases, create a security incident.

Teams

These are the teams that are involved in this interaction and what they want to achieve.

Service team

"I want to understand the security context of the information I will be handling during Alpha."

Information assurance team

"I want to understand what the Service Team want to achieve so that I can provide the best advice."

Objectives

The objective of this interaction is to ensure that the nature of the information under discussion is properly understood and articulated, so that decisions on how to handle it during Alpha can be made.

Activities

The Service Team and Information Assurance function will work together on the following activities.

Initial information assurance assessment

Understanding the value of the information that the service handles and classifying it accordingly.

The Information Assurance function will help the Service Team to ask the right questions during Discovery so as to properly identify the security needs.

By classifying the information based on user need, the Information Assurance function can give clear guidance on what controls will be necessary for Alpha.

Timing

This interaction should be started early in the Discovery phase. This is so that the team have plenty of time to find answers to the questions that will give the security classification.

This initial assessment will help to understand the effort required in Alpha, but further assessment will take place during Alpha and Beta to better understand the security needs.
