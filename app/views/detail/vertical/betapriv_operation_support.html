Private beta operation support

During the Beta phase the Service Team will open up their service to a limited number of users for a Private Beta, to see how well their service works in practice.

The Operation Support function will work with the Service Team to assess the performance of the operating environments during the Private Beta.

Without this interaction, the Service Team will be unable to ready their operating environments for Public Beta.

Teams

These are the teams that are involved in this interaction and what they want to achieve.

Service team

"I want to get real world feedback on my service."

"I want my service to be supported in Private Beta."

"I want to be able to make quick changes to my service in response to user feedback."

Operation support team

"I want to get real world monitoring information on the service."

"I want to apply appropriate monitoring and controls."

Objectives

The objective of this interaction is to ensure that the service team are able to run a Private Beta.

The Operation support team gain an understanding of the real world use of the applications.

Activities

The service team and operation support function will work together on the following activities.

Provision environments

Stand up environments that are required by the service team through scripted provisioning.

Continuous deployment

Continuous deployment including promotion from development to production environments.

The teams should be deploying changes to the service during the Private Beta.

Manage environments

Monitoring and controlling the environments.

Timing

This interaction should carry over from development and so will have already started at the beginning of the Private Beta.
