Defra Service Delivery Lifecycle Definitions

In order to provide a shared understanding of the service delivery lifecycle, some common terminology needs to be agreed.

This section provides the definitions for some particular terms that might either be open to interpretation or tend to have different meanings depending on the context in which they are used.

Service

A Service is the service that Defra provides to its customers

An Application is an IT capability that provides some functionality to a user

Operation Support is all the activities around an application that are provided by people so as to ensure its correct operation

Customer

A Customer is any of the people described in the Defra Group Target Operating Model as customers

A User is anyone, Customer or not, who uses an Application

Agile

An Agile delivery approach is one that is able to respond quickly and positively to change

An Iterative delivery approach is one that allows for and adapts to change by improving through a series of short iterations based on regular feedback

Buy or Build

To Buy is to take a decision that commits to a significant technical or financial investment in a pre-existing application

Wherever possible, such an investment will be made for the benefit of the organisation as a whole, when the application is referred to as a Common Component

To Build is to create an application over time, where investment can be halted at any point

Live

A service is Live when it is in a state of continuous improvement based on operational measurements

An application is in Operation whenever it has the appropriate level of support

Governance

Governance is the act of ensuring that a service is aligned with strategy and policy

Assurance is the act of ensuring that a service is compliant with standards and procedures
